The Effects of Personality on Cognitive Performance Participant Debrief Form

Thank you for taking part in this study

Background

This study investigates the effects of camouflaging social difficulties associated with social interactions on cognitive measures including traits of mental health. Increased use of camouflaging behaviours has been associated with increase anxiety and depression (Hull et al., 2021). Behaviours which attempt to hide autistic traits have been described as cognitively demanding (Livingston et al., 2020) which may lead to cognitive fatigue. Cognitive fatigue has been associated with alterations in cognitive, behavioural and emotional physiological responses (Ream & Richardson, 1996). This study aimed to assess whether personality traits associated with autism resulted in differences in performance on cognitive tasks over time.

Your Data

All person identifiable information you have provided will be stored in an encrypted folder on a secure server at the University of Bath. Once you have completed the lab session all person identifiable information will be destroyed and your data will be fully anonymous. If you wish to withdraw your data you may do so up to the point of completing the lab session, as after this point we will not be able to identify it as yours. The anonymous data will be stored on a secure drive at the University of Bath and only be accessible to researchers and will be stored for a period of 10 years. Your data will only be used for the purpose of research and may be used in future research projects. Any presentation or publication of the data you have provided will not include identifying information.
